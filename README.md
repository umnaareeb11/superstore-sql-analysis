# SQL Sales Analysis Assignment

## Project Overview
This project focuses on performing real-world sales data
analysis using structured SQL queries. The dataset is
based on the Superstore Sales structure from Kaggle.
Queries cover everything from basic filtering to advanced
joins, aggregations, and business case solutions.
All queries are written and executed in MySQL Workbench.

## Work Summary
In this assignment, I worked with a sales dataset containing
orders, customers, products, regions, and profit data.
I wrote 10+ SQL queries to uncover meaningful business
insights — such as identifying top-performing regions,
most profitable product categories, customer segments
driving maximum revenue, and the real impact of
discounts on overall sales performance.

## Assignment Structure
- Section A : Explored table structure, column types
              and counted total rows in the dataset
- Section B : Applied WHERE clause to filter data by
              region, category, and discount range
- Section C : Used GROUP BY with SUM and AVG to find
              total sales by category, region, segment
- Section D : Performed INNER JOIN and LEFT JOIN across
              orders and customer tables
- Section E : Wrote CASE statements to classify orders
              by profit margin and discount level
- Section F : Solved real business problems — monthly
              sales trends, top 5 customers by revenue,
              and least profitable sub-categories

## Analysis Results
- Furniture category has high sales but very low profit margin
- Tables sub-category often results in negative profit (loss)
- Products with zero discount have the highest profit margins
- Consumer segment contributes the most revenue overall
- Phones and Chairs are the top selling sub-categories
- Copiers have the highest profit per unit sold
